Case Study

Welcome.Salesforce.com

Role Lead Product Designer
Team 2 designers, 3 engineers, 1 PM
Timeline 2023 – 2024
Overview

The first screen every new admin sees.

Redesigned the first-touch onboarding experience for new Salesforce customers — the very first screen admins see after their org is provisioned.

The Challenge

From excitement to confusion in minutes.

The existing welcome experience was a generic landing page with links to documentation. New customers reported feeling overwhelmed, unsure what to do first, and unable to connect their business goals to platform capabilities. First-week engagement was declining.

Process

Personalized paths to first value.

1

Research

Interviewed 30 new admins within their first 90 days. Mapped the emotional journey from excitement to confusion to frustration. Identified the "day 2 problem" — the dropoff after initial setup excitement fades.

2

Personalization

Designed an onboarding flow that adapts based on org type (Sales Cloud, Service Cloud, etc.), admin experience level, and company size. No two welcome experiences are the same.

3

Guided Setup

Created a task-based checklist that prioritizes quick wins. Each task includes context (why this matters), estimated time, and a direct deep-link into Setup. Progress persists across sessions.

4

Measurement

Tracked time-to-first-value, checklist completion rates, and 30-day retention. Iterated based on drop-off analysis at each onboarding step.

Results

A better first impression at scale.
