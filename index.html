About
The human behind
the platform.

Hi, I'm Tori — a lead product designer who designs for platforms, simplifies complexity, and champions the humans behind every screen.

With a Master's in Human-Computer Interaction and a decade in the enterprise space, I specialize in the hard stuff: admin consoles, AI configuration flows, automation diagnostics — the technical surfaces that most designers shy away from. I make them feel approachable without dumbing them down.

At Salesforce, I own design for AI-powered setup tools, enterprise search, and platform frameworks used by hundreds of thousands of organizations. My work lives at the intersection of complex backend logic and the admins who need it to just make sense.

Outside of work, I'm a mom of two small kiddos — which means I've mastered the art of designing under pressure, negotiating with tiny stakeholders, and finding clarity in chaos.

Design philosophy
Principles I design by.

Systems Thinking

Technical platforms are ecosystems, not screens. I design components that hold their own while serving the architecture around them.

Radical Empathy

Admins are people, not personas. I sit with the workarounds, the tribal knowledge, and the silent frustrations before I open a design tool.

Inclusive by Default

Complex doesn't have to mean exclusive. I design admin tools that serve power users and newcomers from the same surface.

Measure What Matters

Craft without metrics is decoration. I pair research insight with platform telemetry to ship designs that move real outcomes.

A letter to Slack
Why Slack is my next chapter.

I've seen the power of the Salesforce Platform from the inside, but I've also seen how easily a unique culture can be diluted by enterprise gravity. I'm joining Slack because I align with its mission of being the central hub for real-time collaboration, but I'm equally committed to letting Slack keep its character.

As a strong communicator, I can navigate the 'Salesforce-speak' and bridge the technical patterns without letting the corporate overhead stifle Slack's agility or its voice. I want to build the bridge, not a tunnel that loses the scenery.
